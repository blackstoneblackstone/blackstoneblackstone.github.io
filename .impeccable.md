## Design Context

### Users
The site serves Chinese-speaking developers, AI programming practitioners, full-stack engineers, and indie hackers who come to read technical articles, compare AI coding tools, discover useful projects, and evaluate courses or resource lists.

### Brand Personality
Clear, seasoned, and pragmatic. The interface should feel like a well-kept technical notebook from an experienced builder: calm enough for reading, structured enough for scanning, and distinctive enough to avoid a generic Bootstrap blog feel.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial knowledge base with a light, ink-on-paper foundation, precise dividers, restrained surfaces, and a few confident accent colors. The design should emphasize readable Chinese typography, tool/resource discovery, and fast navigation rather than marketing decoration.

### Design Principles
- Preserve content exactly while improving hierarchy, spacing, rhythm, and interaction quality.
- Favor dense but breathable layouts for repeated reading and comparison.
- Keep cards purposeful: use them for resources and courses, not for every section.
- Use restrained motion and hover states to clarify affordances without distracting from reading.
- Maintain responsive behavior so mobile users can browse articles, tools, and courses without losing content.
